# Corporation: Valley Trust

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Prelude
- **Starting MC:** 37
- **Tags:** Science
- **First Action:** Draw 3 Prelude cards and play 1 of them (discard the others).
- **Effect:** You pay 2 MC less for any card with a Science tag.

## Strategic Analysis (The Prelude Master)
**Status: A-TIER / VERSATILE**

Valley Trust is the only corporation that can directly manipulate the Prelude phase. This versatility, combined with a powerful science discount, makes it a top-tier choice for engine builders.

### Why it is a viable play:
1. **Triple Prelude:** Starting with 3 Preludes instead of 2 (via the first action) gives Valley Trust a massive setup advantage. You effectively get to pick the best of 3 *extra* options.
2. **Science Discount:** Science tags are the gateway to the most powerful late-game cards (*AI Central*, *Quantum Extractor*). A -2 MC discount makes these engines much easier to jumpstart.
3. **Science Tag Start:** Having a science tag on the corp card helps meet requirements for cards like *Research* or *Scientists' Guild* immediately.

### Synergies:
- **AI Central / Mars University:** The discount and the starting tag make these cards your primary targets.
- **Research Outpost:** A science-building hybrid that becomes very cheap.
- **Quantum Extractor / Mass Converter:** These "Requirement: 4 Science" cards are significantly easier to reach.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
Strong for 'Terraformer'. High starting capital allows for early milestone locking.
### Hellas
Good for 'Diversifier' if you can branch into different productions early.
### Elysium
High synergy with 'Legend' milestone due to frequent event plays.

### Tips
- **Prelude Choice:** When using your first action, prioritize production or card draw. Don't just take a "fun" Prelude; take the one that defines your engine.
- **The Science Trap:** Don't play bad science cards just because they are discounted. Science is a means to an end (draws and requirements), not an end in itself.

**Recommendation:** High priority if you have high-requirement science cards in your opening hand.
