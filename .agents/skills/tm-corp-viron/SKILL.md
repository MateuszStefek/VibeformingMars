# Corporation: Viron

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Venus Next
- **Starting MC:** 48
- **Tags:** Microbe
- **Action:** Once per generation, you may use an action on a blue card that has already been used this generation.

## Strategic Analysis (The Action Doubler)
**Status: S-TIER / ELITE**

Viron is widely considered one of the strongest corporations in the game because its ability scales exponentially with the quality of blue cards in play. It doesn't just build an engine; it doubles it.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unmatched Scaling:** Viron starts okay, but becomes unstoppable once it finds a premier blue action.
2. **Card Draw King:** Doubling *AI Central* (draw 4 cards) or *Mars University* triggers makes Viron the most consistent card-drawing threat.
3. **Versatility:** Can double production actions, resource-gathering actions (microbes/animals), or even VP actions.

### Synergies:
- **AI Central:** The "Dream Scenario." Drawing 4 cards a turn usually wins the game.
- **Physics Complex:** 4 VPs per turn for 12 energy. A terrifying point engine.
- **Septem Tribus (via Merger):** In Turmoil, doubling the delegate income action is game-breaking.
- **Water Splitting Plant / Electro Catapult:** Excellent for early-game economy and terraforming.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
High priority for 'Mayor' and 'Gardener'. Use the early game to claim the double-ocean placement bonus for cards/plants.
### Hellas
Relevance of the South Pole for heat bonuses. Target 'Rim Settler' if playing space tags, or 'Polar Explorer' for easy adjacency points.
### Elysium
Focus on the 3-plant placement bonus. 'Generalist' is an easy milestone if you can balance production early.

### Tips
- **Drafting for Actions:** When playing Viron, your drafting priority shifts heavily toward "Action:" cards. Even "B-Tier" actions become "A-Tier" for you.
- **The Power of "Use Again":** Remember that you can use the same action twice in *one* turn (taking two actions), or spread it across the generation.

**Recommendation:** A premier "Tier 0" corporation. If you see Viron and have any decent blue card in your starting hand, it's almost always the correct pick.
