# Project: Heat Trappers (#154)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 6 MC
- **Tags:** Power, Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease any player's Heat production 2 steps. Increase your Energy production 1 step.
- **VP:** -1


## Strategic Analysis (The Anti-Rush Disruptor)
**Status: B-TIER DISRUPTION / S-TIER IN 2-PLAYER GAMES**

Heat Trappers is a high-efficiency disruption card designed to stall terraforming rushers. While it carries a VP penalty, its impact on an opponent's heat engine and your own infrastructure makes it a potent tool in competitive play.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional Denial:** For only 6 MC (+3 to draft), you destroy ~12 MC worth of an opponent's investment (2 heat production) while gaining 1 energy production (~7 MC value). This 19 MC resource swing for a 9 MC cost is massive.
2. **Mohole Counter:** The direct counter to early-game heat producers like *Mohole Area*. Playing this in Generation 1-2 can effectively reset an opponent's temperature engine, buying you several generations of time to scale your own economy.
3. **Double Tag Synergy:** Provides both a **Power** and a **Building** tag.
   - **Power Tag:** Helps hit requirements for cards like *Power Supply Consortium* or *AI Central*.
   - **Building Tag:** Allows you to pay for the card entirely with **Steel**, making it effectively "free" in terms of liquid MC.
4. **Robotic Workforce Combo:** A high-level tactic. Copying Heat Trappers with Robotic Workforce destroys another 2 heat production without incurring a second VP penalty, as the copy only duplicates the production/effect, not the static points.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Mandatory Target:** You **must** decrease someone's heat production. If all opponents are at 0, you must hit yourself. Check the production board before drafting.
- **VP Penalty:** In high-VP engine games, the -1 VP can be a significant price. Only play this early where the disruption and energy income can generate more than 1 point of value over time.
- **4-5 Player Dilution:** In large games, attacking one player only helps you and the other 2-3 players who weren't hit. Use it tactically on the leader or a dedicated terraforming rusher.

### Tips
- **Helion Counter:** Specifically ruins **Helion's** ability to use heat as money. Stealing their production not only stalls the TR but also cripples their future spending power.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in Generations 1-3. Essential for 2-player games or if an opponent is building a heat engine. Skip late unless you strictly need a cheap Power/Building tag.
