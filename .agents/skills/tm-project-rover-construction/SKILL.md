# Project: Rover Construction (#038)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 8 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** When any player places a city tile (including this one), gain 2 MegaCredits (MC).
- **VP:** 1


## Strategic Analysis (The Board Passive)
**Status: C-TIER ECONOMY / B-TIER FOR VITOR**

Rover Construction is a low-cost passive income card that rewards a crowded board. While it once was highly valued for its "unlimited" potential, modern high-level play classifies it as a situational "steel dump" or milestone enabler rather than a core economic engine.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Steel Sink Points:** As a Building card, the 8 MC cost can be paid entirely with Steel. In the final generations, this is an extremely efficient way to convert 4 surplus metal into 1 VP.
2. **Tharsis Republic Synergy:** The absolute best case. If you or your opponent are playing Tharsis, cities will hit the board rapidly, potentially netting you 10-14+ MC over the course of the game.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Vitor:** As a card with a non-negative VP icon, it triggers Vitor's 3 MC rebate. Combined with Steel, this makes the card effectively "profitable" to play.
   - **Pets / Immigrant City:** Forms a "City Engine" where every city placed grants you MC, production, and animals simultaneously.
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a cheap Building tag for the **Builder** milestone or the **Contractor** award.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Early Game Stalling:** Spending 11 MC (cost + draft) in Generation 1-2 for passive income you cannot control is often a mistake. That capital is better spent on reliable production like *Acquired Company*. You need **6 cities** to be placed globally just to break even on the investment.
- **2-Player Inefficiency:** In 1v1 games, cities are often sparse. If fewer than 4 cities are placed after you play the card, it statistically loses money compared to other point options.
- **Opportunity Cost:** If you aren't playing with Vitor or Tharsis, skip this card in early drafts to preserve your budget for high-impact engine pieces.

### Tips
- **Stealth Award Swap:** In the final turn, use the 2 MC gain from a late city placement to fund an award that an opponent thought you were too poor to afford.

**Recommendation:** A low-priority draft for most players. High priority if playing **Vitor**, **Tharsis**, or in a 4-5 player city-heavy match. Excellent as a final point dump for excess Steel. Skip early in 2-player games.
