# Project: Immigrant City (#200)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 13 MC
- **Tags:** City, Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease your Energy production 1 step. Decrease your MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps. Increase your MC production 1 step for each City tile in play (including this one).
- **VP:** 1 VP per adjacent Greenery.


## Strategic Analysis (The Board Passive)
**Status: S-TIER ON THARSIS / B-TIER GENERAL**

Immigrant City is a unique and powerful economic card that rewards players for a crowded board. While it starts with a significant economic penalty, its ability to "leech" production from every subsequent city placement makes it a game-defining card in the right matchups.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional Scaling:** It provides +1 MC production for *every* city placed by *any* player. In high-player games (4-5 players), it can easily reach +8-10 MC production, making it more efficient than almost any other city card.
2. **Tharsis Republic Synergy:** The absolute best case. It effectively doubles Tharsis's passive income from cities, creating an economic engine that is nearly impossible for opponents to match.
3. **Cheap City for Milestones:** At only 13 MC (+3 to draft), it is one of the cheapest city placements in the game. This is critical for securing the **Mayor** milestone or grabbing high-value placement bonuses early.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Rover Construction / Pets:** These cards create a "Board Engine" where every city placed grants you MC, production, and plants simultaneously.
   - **Building Tag:** Can be paid for with Steel, potentially making the card "free" in terms of liquid MC.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Early Game Deficit:** If you play this in Generation 1 and no one else builds cities, you are at -1 net MC production. You need **three total cities** on the board just to break even on production. Do not play this early unless you see multiple city-heavy opponents (like Tharsis) or have the cities in your own hand to fuel it.
- **Deterrent Effect:** Playing Immigrant City early can actively discourage opponents from building cities, potentially "starving" your card of its value.
- **Energy Cost:** Remember to account for the 1 energy production (~7 MC value).

### Tips
- **Late-Game Point Dump:** In the final 2 generations, the MC production penalty doesn't matter. Treat Immigrant City as a low-cost 13 MC city to grab final victory points and adjacency bonuses.

**Recommendation:** A "Must-Keep" for Tharsis Republic or if holding *Rover Construction*. High priority in 4-5 player games. In 2-player games, treat it as a situational tool for milestone racing. Skip late if you have no energy.
