# Project: Aquifer Pumping (#052)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 18 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Action:** Spend 8 MC to place 1 Ocean tile.


## Strategic Analysis (The Steel Sink)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER FOR MINING GUILD**

Aquifer Pumping is a situational card that is often considered a "trap" due to its high upfront cost. However, it excels as a conversion tool for players with excessive building resources and is a cornerstone for specific corporations.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Steel Conversion:** Its greatest strength is that the 8 MC action cost **can be paid with Steel**. If you have high steel production but no building tags to spend it on, this card allows you to convert "dead" metal into TR, placement bonuses, and points.
2. **Mining Guild Synergy:** The premier card for **Mining Guild**. It provides a repeatable way to trigger the corporation's passive (gaining steel production when placing a tile on a bonus) while utilizing the steel the corp already generates.
3. **Solo Mode Essential:** In Solo play, where placing all 9 oceans is mandatory, Aquifer Pumping becomes a high-efficiency tool over 7+ generations, providing a massive discount over standard project oceans.
4. **Ceres (Colonies):** If the **Ceres** colony is in play, steel becomes abundant. Aquifer Pumping serves as the perfect "dump" for this excess resource.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Poor Raw Math:** To break even against the 18 MC Standard Project (SP) ocean, you must use this card **three times**. (18 cost + 3 buy + 8 action = 29 MC for the first ocean vs. 18 MC for SP). You only start saving money on the 4th use.
- **Ocean Rushing:** In 4-5 player games, oceans disappear quickly. If you invest 21 MC (cost + draft) early, but the oceans are filled by other players before you can use the action 3+ times, you have lost a significant amount of capital.
- **Opportunity Cost:** The 21 MC investment in Generation 1-2 is usually better spent on long-term production (MC, Science, or Earth tags) that compounds throughout the game.

### Tips
- **Adjacency Snipe:** Use the repeatable action to "walk" your way to high-value ocean bonuses (like 2 titanium or 2 cards) before opponents can reach them.

**Recommendation:** A low-priority draft for most players. High priority if playing **Mining Guild** or **Solo**. Only keep in multiplayer if you have 3+ steel production and no other outlets. Skip in high-player count "Rush" games.
