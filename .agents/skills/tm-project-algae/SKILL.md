# Project: Algae (#051)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 10 MC
- **Tags:** Plant
- **Requirements:** 5 Ocean Tiles
- **Effect:** Increase your Plant production 2 steps. Gain 1 Plant.


## Strategic Analysis (The Mid-Game Scaler)
**Status: A-TIER PRODUCTION / B-TIER TIMING**

Algae is a high-efficiency plant production card that is strictly limited by the board state. While it offers a fantastic return on investment, its 5-ocean requirement often dictates its role as a mid-game engine booster rather than an early-game starter.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional Efficiency:** For 10 MC (+3 to draft), you gain 2 plant production and 1 instant plant. This is a ~5.5 MC per production ratio, significantly better than *Heather* or *Adapted Lichen*.
2. **Double Production:** Getting 2 steps of plant production in one action allows you to bypass the slow "drip" of single-step cards and start placing greeneries every 4 generations.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Ocean-Heavy Preludes:** If you or your opponents play ocean-placing Preludes (e.g., *Great Aquifer*), Algae can come online as early as Generation 2-3.
   - **Insects / NRA:** Provides a Plant tag and the necessary production to meet the high-tier biological requirements.
   - **Viral Enhancers:** Increases the instant plant gain to 2, accelerating your next greenery.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Requirement Bottleneck:** In games where oceans are slow (common in 2-player engine games), Algae can sit in your hand for half the game, wasting 13 MC of potential capital. **Do not draft it early** unless you see multiple ocean-focused corporations or Preludes at the table.
- **Predator Vulnerability:** A sudden spike to +2 plant production will draw the attention of players holding *Birds* or *Virus*. Be ready to convert your plants to greeneries immediately.

### Tips
- **Final Click Value:** Unlike *Arctic Algae*, this card remains useful in the final generations. The 2 production + 1 instant plant can often provide the exact amount of plants needed to sneak in one last greenery before the game ends.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in Generations 3-5 once the 5-ocean threshold is in sight. Essential for *Ecoline* or bio-focused builds. Skip early if no one is placing oceans.
