# Project: Soletta (#176)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 35 MC
- **Tags:** Space
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your Heat production 7 steps.


## Strategic Analysis (The Game-Pacer)
**Status: S-TIER RUSH TOOL / A-TIER FOR HELION**

Soletta is the single most powerful heat production card in the game. It allows one player to control the entire pace of the match by forcing the temperature track to finish early, effectively "starving" engine-builders of the generations they need to make their setups profitable.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled "Rush" Potential:** +7 Heat production means you are raising the temperature almost every single generation. In a standard game, this single card can shorten the match by 2-3 generations, which is often the difference between winning and losing against a science/Jovian engine.
2. **Space Tag Utility:** As a Space card, it can be paid for entirely with Titanium. This is the premier way for titanium-rich players or corporations like **Phobolog** to "dump" their metals into immediate game control.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Helion:** The absolute best case. Since Helion can use Heat as MC, Soletta effectively becomes a **+7 MC production card** for a very low net cost.
   - **Specialist / Thermalist:** Almost guarantees securing the **Specialist** milestone (highest production) and the **Thermalist** award.
   - **Credicor:** Triggers the 4 MC rebate for high-cost cards.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **"Gen 1 or Never" Rule:** The consensus in high-level play is that Soletta should be played in **Generation 1 or 2**. By Generation 4, the 38 MC investment (cost + draft) will rarely have enough time to pay back in TR and heat bonuses.
- **Economic Sacrifice:** Playing Soletta in Gen 1 often requires skipping every other draft pick and potentially selling cards. Ensure your starting corporation (e.g., *Credicor*, *Manutech*) can handle the tempo hit.
- **Colonies Trade-off:** In games with *Colonies*, the 35 MC might be better spent on a Trade Fleet or the *Io* colony, which provides heat production more flexibly.

### Tips
- **Unity Party (Turmoil):** Prioritize playing this when the **Unity** party is ruling to maximize the value of the Titanium you spend to buy it.
- **Slot Efficiency:** While slightly less efficient per unit than *Mohole Area*, Soletta is "slot efficient"—it provides massive production in a single card/action, saving you from needing to draft and play 3 separate heat cards.

**Recommendation:** A top-tier "Auto-Keep" if you can play it in Generation 1-2, especially as **Helion** or **Phobolog**. Priority 1 for "Rush" strategies. Skip after Generation 4.
