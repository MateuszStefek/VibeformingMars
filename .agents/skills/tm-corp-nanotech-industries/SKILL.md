# Corporation: Nanotech Industries

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Moon
- **Starting MC:** 42
- **Starting Tags:** Science, Moon
- **First Action:** Draw 3 cards, keep 2, discard 1.
- **Action:** Add 1 science resource to ANY card (except those giving 2+ VP per science resource).
- **VP:** 1 per 2 science resources on this card.

## Strategic Analysis (The Science Enabler)
**Status: A-TIER (Science Synergy)**

Nanotech Industries is a powerful engine-building corporation that excels in games where science tags and resource-stacking cards are prevalent. Its ability to feed science resources to other cards makes it a versatile support piece for some of the game's most powerful VP engines.

### Why it is a strong play:
1. **Strong Start:** Drawing 3 cards and keeping 2 for your first action provides excellent early-game flexibility and helps find key science payoffs.
2. **Resource Versatility:** The ability to add science resources to *any* card (with minor restrictions) is massive. This can accelerate 'AI Central', 'Search for Life', or any expansion cards that use science resources.
3. **Internal VP Engine:** Even if you have no other cards to feed, you can stack resources on Nanotech itself for a respectable VP payout (1 VP per 2 resources).

### Synergies:
- **AI Central / Search for Life:** Directly feeds these powerful base-game engines.
- **Science Payoffs:** Any card that scales with Science tags or resources.
- **Mars University / Olympus Conference:** The starting Science tag helps trigger these draw engines immediately.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
Standard play. Focus on efficiency and building your science engine.
### Hellas / Elysium
Look for milestones that reward card play or tags (e.g., 'Legend' or 'Scientist').

### Tips
- **Don't Forget the Action:** It's easy to focus on your own VP, but feeding an 'AI Central' or a powerful expansion card is usually worth more than 0.5 VP per turn.
- **First Action Priority:** Use your first action to draw cards before making other big decisions in Generation 1.

**Recommendation:** A top-tier pick if you have science-dependent cards in your starting hand. Extremely flexible and scales well into the late game.
