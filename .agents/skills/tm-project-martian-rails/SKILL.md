# Project: Martian Rails (#007)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 13 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Action:** Spend 1 Energy to gain 1 MC for each City tile on Mars.


## Strategic Analysis (The City-Dependent Engine)
**Status: C-TIER / SITUATIONAL "TRAP" CARD**

Martian Rails is often a "trap" for new players. While it looks like a powerful scaling income source, its high opportunity cost and dependence on an opponent's "ground game" make it inefficient in competitive play.

### Why it is usually a "Skip":
1. **High Break-Even:** With a total cost of 16 MC (13 + 3 to draft) and an energy requirement (valued at ~7 MC), you need to generate ~25 MC just to pay for the card's existence.
2. **Slow Startup:** In competitive games, players prioritize terraforming over cities. If there are only 2 cities on the board in Generation 5, the Rails are losing you money.
3. **Action Cost:** It consumes an action and 1 energy every turn. That energy is often better spent on card draw (e.g., *AI Central*) or oxygen production.
4. **Dependence:** You are relying on your opponents to build cities. If they switch to a "Space Engine" or "Bio Rush," your Rails will never become profitable.

### When to Play It:
- **Tharsis Republic in Play:** Tharsis's strategy forces cities onto the board, making the Rails much more likely to hit the 5+ city threshold for profitability.
- **Mid-Game Steel Dump:** If you have 20+ steel and no other building tags to spend it on, the Rails can be a "boring but practical" way to turn that steel into liquid MC for the final turns.
- **Energy Surplus:** If you have excess energy from *Colonies* or *Preludes* and no other way to spend it, the Rails can provide a steady trickle of cash.
- **Mayor Race:** In a game where 3+ players are rushing cities for the *Mayor* milestone.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Draft Timing:** Do not draft this in Generations 1-3 unless you are playing Tharsis or see 3+ city-heavy corporations at the table.
- **Math Check:** If the current city count is below 4, the action is barely worth the energy it consumes. Wait until the board is crowded.

### Tips
- **Stalling:** Like other action cards, use Martian Rails as your first action in a turn to "pass time" and see if opponents commit to a specific board area or global bonus before you make your big move.

**Recommendation:** Generally a "Hard Pass." Only keep if you have a massive steel surplus and see a high city count developing by Generation 5. Priority 1 is to spend energy on card draw instead.
