# Corporation: Terralabs Research

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Turmoil
- **Starting MC:** 14
- **Starting TR:** -1 (Starts at 19 TR)
- **Tags:** Science, Earth
- **Effect:** Buying cards to your hand (including the starting hand) costs 1 MC each instead of 3 MC.

## Strategic Analysis (The Hyper-Efficient Researcher)
**Status: C-TIER / HIGH VARIANCE**

Terralabs Research is the glass cannon of Terraforming Mars. It has the lowest starting capital and a TR penalty, but its card-drafting efficiency is unmatched. It wins by seeing the entire deck and playing only the absolute best cards.

### Why it is a viable play:
1. **1 MC Drafting:** Paying only 1 MC for cards allows Terralabs to keep almost everything it sees in the draft, creating an enormous hand of options.
2. **Science/Earth Start:** Starting with two of the best tags in the game helps with requirements and early economic cards.
3. **Scaling:** In long games (10+ generations), the savings from drafting can exceed 100 MC.

### Synergies:
- **Card-Heavy Strategies:** *Mars University*, *Olympus Conference*, and *AI Central* turn your massive hand into even more resources.
- **Pluto Colony:** If you can trade here, your hand size will become unmanageable for opponents.
- **Preludes:** You *need* economic Preludes (*Allied Bank*, *Acquired Company*) to survive the first 2 generations with only 14 MC.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
High priority for 'Mayor' and 'Gardener'. Use the early game to claim the double-ocean placement bonus for cards/plants.
### Hellas
Relevance of the South Pole for heat bonuses. Target 'Rim Settler' if playing space tags, or 'Polar Explorer' for easy adjacency points.
### Elysium
Focus on the 3-plant placement bonus. 'Generalist' is an easy milestone if you can balance production early.

### Tips
- **Survive the Early Game:** Your first priority is not losing in Gen 2. Use your starting tags to play cheap income cards.
- **Draft Aggressively:** Keep every card that has even a marginal chance of being played. At 1 MC, the cost of keeping a card is negligible.

**Recommendation:** Very strong in 2-player games where the pace is slower. Risky in 4-5 player games where you might get out-tempoed before your savings kick in.
