# Project: Pets (#171)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 10 MC
- **Tags:** Earth, Animal
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps (Wait, search said no production? Let me re-verify.)

*(Correction: Effect: Whenever any player places a city tile, add 1 Animal to this card. Comes with 1 Animal.)*
- **VP:** 1 VP per 2 Animals on this card.


## Strategic Analysis (The Board Passive)
**Status: A-TIER IN 4-5 PLAYER GAMES / B-TIER UTILITY**

Pets is a unique passive scoring card that rewards you for a crowded board. While it provides no economic production, its ability to generate victory points from every city placement (including opponents') makes it a high-value tool for long-form or city-heavy games.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Passive Point Accumulation:** Unlike most animal cards that require an action, Pets scales automatically. In a 4-5 player game, it is common to see 8-12 cities, yielding 4-6+ VP for a total investment of only 13 MC (+3 to draft). 
2. **"Protected" Points:** A unique advantage of Pets is that the animals **cannot be removed** (per official rules, although some digital versions allow it). It is one of the few "safe" places to park victory points that cannot be stolen by *Predators* or destroyed by *Virus*.
3. **Double Tag Catalyst:** Provides both an **Earth** and an **Animal** tag.
   - **Earth:** Triggers *Point Luna* for card draw and *Cartel* for production.
   - **Animal:** Triggers *Ecological Zone* and contributes to the *Ecologist* milestone.
4. **Tharsis Republic Synergy:** The absolute best case. If you or your opponent are playing Tharsis, cities will hit the board rapidly, making Pets an essential point engine.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Early Game Opportunity Cost:** Playing Pets in Generation 1-2 costs 13 MC that could have gone toward production. Only play early if you have excess cash or zero other production options. Its ideal play window is Generation 3-4, right before the "city rush" begins.
- **2-Player Inefficiency:** In 1v1 games, cities are often sparse. If fewer than 5 cities are placed after playing Pets, the card statistically loses value compared to other scoring options.
- **Requirement Rule:** Remember that cities on moons (Phobos/Ganymede) and Venus **do count** for Pets.

### Tips
- **Requirement Bypassing:** Use the Animal tag on Pets to hit the requirement for **Advanced Ecosystems** early. Even if the VP potential on Pets is low, its role as a cheap bio-tag enabler makes it worth the draft.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in 4-5 player games or if playing with **Tharsis Republic**. High priority for **Point Luna**. Skip in slow 2-player games or if Oxygen/Temperature are already near the max.
