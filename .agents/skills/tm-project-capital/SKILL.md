# Project: Capital (#008)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 26 MC
- **Tags:** Building, City
- **Requirements:** 4 Ocean Tiles
- **Effect:** Decrease your Energy production 2 steps. Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 5 steps. Place the Capital City tile.
- **VP:** 1 VP for each adjacent Ocean tile. 1 VP for each adjacent Greenery tile.


## Strategic Analysis (The Steel Sink Point Bomb)
**Status: C-TIER ENGINE / S-TIER LATE-GAME FINISHER**

Capital is a high-cost, high-reward card that is often a "trap" for newer players. While its income boost looks impressive, its true value lies in its potential as a late-game point bomb and a way to convert excess building resources into victory points.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Steel Conversion:** As a Building card, the entire 26 MC cost can be paid with Steel. In the final generations, this is one of the most efficient ways to "dump" accumulated metal into points.
2. **Exceptional Point Density:** If placed strategically (e.g., adjacent to 3 oceans and 3 greeneries), Capital can be worth **6-7 VP** on a single tile. This is one of the highest point yields for a single card in the game.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Mining Guild:** Triggers the corporation's passive and provides a Building tag for milestones.
   - **Mass Converter / Physics Complex:** Science engines often overproduce energy; Capital provides a way to trade that "dead" production for points and income.
   - **Elysium Map:** High value for the *Estate Dealer* award (tiles next to oceans).

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Poor Amortization:** The card costs ~43 MC total (card + draft + value of 2 energy production). With only +5 MC income, it takes **9 generations** to break even on cash. Since it requires 4 oceans, you can rarely play it before Generation 5, meaning it will **never** pay for itself as an economic engine.
- **Ocean Bottleneck:** You **must** have 4 oceans on the board. In 2-player games or engine-heavy matchups, this requirement can arrive too late to be useful.
- **Colonies Trade-off:** In games with *Colonies*, energy is vital for trading. Spending 2 energy production on a city can cripple your ability to trade for better resources (like cards or titanium).

### Tips
- **Final Generation Finish:** Hold Capital until the very last round. Use it to occupy a spot surrounded by oceans that opponents thought was "safe." The surprise 3-4 VP from ocean adjacency can often decide a close match.

**Recommendation:** Almost always skip in the early-to-mid game. A high-priority draft in the final 2 generations if you have high Steel production or surplus Energy. Focus exclusively on point density rather than income.
