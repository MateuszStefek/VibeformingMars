# Prelude: Donation

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Prelude
- **Effect:** Gain 21 MC.
- **Tags:** None.

## Strategic Analysis (The Pacing Benchmark)
**Status: B-TIER UTILITY / THE FLEXIBILITY GOLD STANDARD**

Donation is the universal benchmark against which all other Preludes are measured. While it provides zero long-term production or tag synergy, its raw liquid capital makes it a premier tool for establishing immediate board dominance or jumpstarting expensive engines.

### Why it is a top-tier pick:
1. **The "21 MC Rule":** In the 2024-2025 meta, 21 MC is considered the "fair value" for a Prelude. Any Prelude providing less than this must offer extreme synergy to be viable. Donation ensures you always receive full value from your start.
2. **Tempo Catalyst:** It provides the liquidity needed to play high-impact Generation 1 cards that would otherwise be too expensive.
   - **AI Central / Earth Catapult:** Donation allows you to drop these game-defining cards on Turn 1 or 2, which is often worth more than +4-6 production over the long term.
   - **Colonies:** Secured a critical **Colony** placement (17 MC) immediately, which is often the most important Gen 1 priority in expansion games.
3. **Bridge for "Poor" Corps:** Essential for corporations with low starting capital but high-impact abilities (e.g., *Phobolog*, *UNMI*, or *Ecoline*). It provides the "grease" needed to actually use their abilities in the first generation.
4. **Information Neutrality:** Unlike tag-based Preludes, Donation doesn't "telegraph" your strategy. It keeps your options open, allowing you to react to whatever cards you draw in the first few generations.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Zero Tags:** In the modern *Prelude 2* meta, the lack of a tag is a significant opportunity cost. You are missing out on potential rebates and milestone progress.
- **Long-Game Inefficiency:** In 2-player games or solo play (which last 12+ generations), **production Preludes** (like *Allied Bank*) will almost always outperform Donation's flat 21 MC.
- **Rich Corps:** If playing *Teractor* or *Credicor*, you likely don't need the extra cash. Adding 21 MC often results in "over-liquidity" where you have more money than useful cards.

### Tips
- **Milestone Snipe:** Use the 21 MC to fund a rapid expansion toward a milestone (like *Generalist* or *Builder*) before opponents can react. Immediate cash is the hardest resource for opponents to predict and block.

**Recommendation:** A solid "Safe" pick. High priority if your starting hand is expensive or if you need to secure a Gen 1 colony. Skip in long games in favor of production. Skip if your corporation already has high starting capital.
