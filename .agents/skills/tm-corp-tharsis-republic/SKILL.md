# Corporation: Tharsis Republic

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Starting MC:** 40
- **Ability 1 (On Play):** Place a city tile on Mars.
- **Ability 2 (Ongoing):** Whenever a city tile is placed on Mars (by you or others), increase your MC production 1 step.
- **Ability 3 (Ongoing):** Whenever *you* place a city tile on Mars, you get 3 MC back.

## Strategic Analysis (The Board Dominator)
**Status: A-TIER (4-5 Players) / B-TIER (2 Players)**

Tharsis Republic is the master of the board and the undisputed king of city placement. It thrives in high-player games where the board becomes crowded quickly, turning every city placement into income.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Immediate Production:** Starting with a city (which grants +1 MC production) and the corporation ability means Tharsis starts with at least +2 MC production in Gen 1.
2. **Passive Scaling:** In 4-5 player games, opponents *must* build cities to score points, which directly fuels your economy.
3. **Milestone Domination:** Tharsis is the primary candidate for 'Mayor' and 'Gardener' milestones.

### Synergies:
- **Research Outpost:** Reduces card costs and places a city, triggering all Tharsis abilities.
- **Rover Construction:** Grants 2 MC every time a tile is placed, adding to the efficiency of city/greenery chains.
- **Immigrant City / Pets:** Forms the "City Engine" where every city placement grants multiple benefits.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
Ideal for 'Mayor'. The dense ocean clusters are perfect for maximizing city/greenery points.
### Hellas
Placement bonuses in the south are key for early economy.
### Elysium
The 'Estate Dealer' award is highly competitive; secure ocean adjacencies early.

### Tips
- **Avoid Over-Expansion:** Don't build cities just for the sake of it. You need greeneries to make your cities worth points.
- **Counter-Play:** Opponents may try to "stall" city placement or rush terraforming to end the game before your city engine fully matures.
- **Positioning:** Use your starting city to claim high-value spots (e.g., near oceans or high-metal areas) to block opponents.

**Recommendation:** A top pick for 4-5 player games. High priority if you have city cards or 'Rover Construction' in your starting hand.
