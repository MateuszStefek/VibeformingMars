## Strategic Analysis (The Early Game Dig)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / SITUATIONAL "PANIC" DRAW**

Shanty Town is a low-cost utility card used primarily to cycle through your deck when your starting hand is weak. While it provides a modest economic boost, its conditional draw requirement makes it a high-skill "feast or famine" play.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Cheap Card Cycling:** For only 7 MC (+3 to draft), it is one of the most affordable ways to draw 2 cards. This helps you "dig" deeper into the deck to find win-conditions or milestones without the high cost of more premium draw cards like *AI Central*.
2. **Economic Efficiency:** The +1 MC production is a solid "standard project" value for the 10 MC total investment. It ensures the card isn't a complete loss if the draw condition is met.
3. **Steel Sink:** As a Building card, it can be paid for with Steel. This makes it a very efficient way to convert early-game metal into card advantage and income.
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a Building tag toward the **Builder** milestone or **Contractor** award.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **The "Empty Hand" Trap:** To trigger the draw, Shanty Town **must be the last card in your hand**. If you are holding high-cost engine pieces you can't afford to play yet, Shanty Town becomes a dead draw or a weak +1 production card.
- **Order of Operations:** You **must** play every other card in your hand *before* playing Shanty Town. If you draw into cards you want to keep, you've successfully triggered the engine; if you draw into cards you can't play, you've at least filtered the deck.
- **Speculation Risk:** **Do not hold this from Generation 1** if you have a hand full of good production cards. Tying up 3 MC for a card you might not be able to trigger for 4-5 generations will stall your economic snowball.

### Tips
- **Mid-Generation "Pass":** Use Shanty Town as your first action in a generation only if your hand is already empty. This allows you to draw 2 cards and potentially take a third and fourth action in the same turn, catching opponents off guard who thought you were done for the generation.

**Recommendation:** A low-to-mid priority draft. Keep only if you are playing a "hand-dump" strategy (e.g., *Credicor* or *Manutech*) or are desperate for card draw in a weak opening hand. Skip early if you have solid production options.
