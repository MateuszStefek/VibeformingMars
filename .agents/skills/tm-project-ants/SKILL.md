# Project: Ants (#047)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 9 MC
- **Tags:** Microbe
- **Requirements:** 4% Oxygen
- **Action:** Remove 1 Microbe from any card (including this one) and add it to this card.
- **VP:** 1 VP per 2 Microbes on this card.


## Strategic Analysis (The Disruptive Predator)
**Status: B-TIER DISRUPTION / S-TIER IN SPECIFIC MATCHUPS**

Ants is the primary predator for microbe-based engines. While its direct point generation is slow, its value as a disruptive tool and a tempo-stalling action makes it a high-skill ceiling card in competitive play.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Engine Disruption:** Ants is the only direct counter to powerful microbe engines like **GHG Producing Bacteria** or **Regolith Eaters**. By removing their microbes every generation, you can effectively stall an opponent's TR or income growth.
2. **Tempo Stalling:** In the 2024-2025 meta, Ants is highly valued as a "wait action." Because you can take the action (even if you just move a microbe from Ants to itself) without passing, it allows you to see an opponent's big move before committing your own resources.
3. **VP Efficiency:** At 1 VP per 2 microbes, it is the most efficient microbe-to-VP converter in the game. If you have a reliable way to generate microbes, Ants becomes a very cheap end-game point scorer.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Enceladus (Colonies):** The ultimate pairing. Enceladus allows you to add microbes to Ants without relying on an opponent's board, turning it into a 0.5-1 VP per generation engine.
   - **Decomposers:** Ants can "harvest" the microbes gained by Decomposers every generation, creating a closed-loop point engine.
   - **Viral Enhancers:** Triggers off the Microbe tag and provides immediate plant resources.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Oxygen Barrier:** The 4% Oxygen requirement means you cannot play it in the very early game. Do not draft it in Generation 1 unless you have a path to rapid Oxygen growth or are playing against a known microbe corporation (*Splice*).
- **Drafting Logic:** Ants is often a "hate-draft." Even if you don't intend to play it, keeping it away from a player who has a microbe engine is frequently worth the 3 MC cost.

### Tips
- **Targeting Decomposers:** If an opponent has Decomposers, they are gaining microbes every time *anyone* plays a bio-tag. Use Ants to steal those microbes for yourself, turning their engine payoff into your own victory points.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority "hate-draft" if opponents have microbe tags. High priority in any game with *Enceladus*. Use tactically for stalling in the final generations. Skip if no one is playing microbes and Enceladus is not in play.
