# Project: Dust Seals (#136)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 2 MC
- **Tags:** None
- **Requirements:** Max 3 Oceans
- **VP:** 1


## Strategic Analysis (The "Meme" Point)
**Status: D-TIER VP FILLER / S-TIER FOR VITOR**

Dust Seals is one of the most situational cards in the game. While it offers a technically high point-to-cost ratio, its strict "Max 3 Oceans" requirement and lack of tags make it difficult to play optimally in a competitive engine.

### Why it is a good play (rarely):
1. **Efficiency for Vitor:** The absolute best case. As **Vitor**, you receive a 3 MC rebate for playing a card with a VP icon. This makes Dust Seals effectively **profitable** (2 MC cost - 3 MC refund = +1 MC gain) for 1 VP.
2. **Milestone Hunting:** A very cheap card to help reach the **Tactician** milestone (Hellas map) or to contribute to the **Administrator** award (most cards with no tags).
3. **Cheap Requirement Bridge:** If drawn for free, it provides a 1 VP boost for almost no capital, helping you spend every last credit in the early generations.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Opportunity Cost:** **Never draft this early.** Spending 3 MC to buy a 2 MC card for 1 VP in Generation 1 is a waste of capital. That 5 MC is much better spent on establishing your economic production.
- **Ocean Bottleneck:** The "Max 3 Oceans" limit is often reached by Generation 2 or 3 in high-player games. Dust Seals frequently becomes a "dead draw" before you have the spare cash to justify playing it.
- **No Tag Value:** Unlike most point cards, Dust Seals has no tags, meaning it provides zero synergy for *Science* requirements, *Diversifier*, or *Ecologist* milestones.

### Tips
- **Requirement Cheating:** If you are playing as **Inventrix** or have **Special Design**, you can extend the window to play Dust Seals up to 5 oceans. This allows you to drop it as a "surprise point" in the mid-game after your core engine is established.

**Recommendation:** Generally an "Easy Pass" in drafts. Only keep if you are **Vitor**, playing for the **Administrator** award, or if you draw it for free in the very first generation. Skip in all other scenarios.
