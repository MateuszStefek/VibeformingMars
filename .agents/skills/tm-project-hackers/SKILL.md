# Project: Hackers (#125)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 3 MC
- **Tags:** Science
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease your Energy production 1 step. Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps. Decrease any player's MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps.
- **VP:** -1


## Strategic Analysis (The Zero-Sum Disruptor)
**Status: F-TIER / SITUATIONAL "HATE-PLAY"**

Hackers is widely regarded as one of the weakest cards in the game. While it offers a theoretical 4-production swing, its high opportunity cost and negative victory point make it nearly impossible to justify in a standard engine build.

### Why it is almost never played:
1. **Inefficient Math:** To play Hackers, you spend 6 MC (buy + play) and sacrifice 1 energy production (~7 MC value). In return, you gain 2 MC production and steal 2 from an opponent. This "swing" costs ~13 MC and results in a -1 VP penalty. Most 11 MC production cards offer better long-term ROI without the point loss.
2. **Energy Bottleneck:** Sacrifice of energy production is a heavy price, especially in the *Colonies* era where energy is needed for trading.
3. **Drafting Trap:** Tying up 3 MC to hold a card that might marginally hurt an opponent in Generation 1 is usually a mistake. That capital is better spent on establishing your own independent engine.

### When to Play It (Pro Tactics):
- **Robinson Industries Counter:** The most effective use of Hackers. Playing this against Robinson Industries in Generation 1-2 can cripple their ability to use their corporation power by forcing their MC production into the negatives.
- **Milestone Snipe:** Use it in the final turn to lower an opponent's production just enough to deny them the **Banker** award.
- **Cheapest Science Key:** If you are desperate for a third Science tag to unlock **AI Central** and have zero other options, Hackers can serve as an expensive 6 MC "tag enabler."

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Multplayer Dilution:** In 4-5 player games, attacking one player benefits everyone else at the table who wasn't involved. Hackers is strictly a 2-player or highly targeted competitive card.

**Recommendation:** Almost always a "Never-Buy." Only keep if you are in a 1v1 match against Robinson Industries or if you are 100% committed to a Science requirement and have no other tags. Skip in all other scenarios.
