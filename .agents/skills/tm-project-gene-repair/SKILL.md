# Project: Gene Repair (#029)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 12 MC
- **Tags:** Science
- **Requirements:** 3 Science Tags
- **Effect:** Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps.
- **VP:** 2


## Strategic Analysis (The Science Bridge)
**Status: A-TIER ENABLER / MID-GAME VALUE**

Gene Repair is a high-efficiency "bridge" card. While its requirements prevent it from being an early-game engine starter, its combination of production, points, and a Science tag makes it a staple for any player pursuing high-tier science payoffs.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Mathematical Efficiency:** Community analysis values 2 VP at ~10 MC and 2 MC production at ~9 MC. For a 12 MC investment (+3 to draft), you are receiving ~22 MC of value, making it one of the most efficient science cards in the game.
2. **The "Payoff" Tag:** It provides a Science tag while simultaneously refunding its cost through production. This is the most efficient way to reach the 5-tag or 7-tag requirements for powerhouses like **Mass Converter** or **Antigravity Technology**.
3. **Scientist Award:** Essential for securing the *Scientist* award or milestone, as it provides both the tag and the points to win a tie-breaker.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Mars University / Olympus Conference:** Triggers these high-volume draw engines for a very low net cost.
   - **Vitor:** As a card with a non-negative VP icon, it triggers Vitor's 3 MC rebate.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Timing Window:** Playing this after Generation 7 is often a mistake. The 2 MC production won't have time to pay off, and you are better off spending your 15 MC on direct terraforming or 1:1 VP cards.
- **Forcing the Requirement:** Do not play "junk" science cards purely to unlock Gene Repair. It is a value card, not a game-ender. Only play it if your engine naturally reaches 3 tags.

### Tips
- **Hate-Drafting:** If you see an opponent with 2 Science tags and a growing engine, you **must** cut Gene Repair. Allowing them to land a tag, production, and points in one action accelerates their path to the game's most oppressive requirements.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft if you have any science production or are pursuing *AI Central*. Best played between Generations 4 and 6. Skip in the very late game.
