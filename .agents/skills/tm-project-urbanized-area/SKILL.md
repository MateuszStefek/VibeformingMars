# Project: Urbanized Area (#051)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 10 MC
- **Tags:** Building, City
- **Requirements:** None (but must be adjacent to at least 2 City tiles).
- **Effect:** Decrease your Energy production 1 step. Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 2 steps. Place a City tile.
- **VP:** 1 VP per adjacent Greenery.


## Strategic Analysis (The City Sneak)
**Status: A-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER BLOCKER**

Urbanized Area is a high-utility board-control card that ignores the standard "two-hex distance" rule for cities. While its requirements limit its early-game use, its ability to "sneak" into crowded city clusters makes it a premier tool for stealing points and securing board milestones.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Rule-Breaking Placement:** Its primary value is placing a city **adjacent to other cities**. This allows you to occupy hexes that opponents thought were "safe" from city point-leeching, or to squeeze a city into a crowded board to secure 3-4 VP from existing greeneries.
2. **Exceptional Cost-to-City Ratio:** At only 10 MC (+3 to draft), it is one of the cheapest city placements in the game. Even accounting for the 1 energy penalty (~7 MC value), the total investment of ~20 MC is significantly cheaper than a 25 MC Standard Project city.
3. **Milestone Sniping:** Use it to "swipe" the **Mayor** milestone (3 cities) in the mid-to-late game. Because it can be placed in city clusters, you can often find a spot for it even when the rest of the board is full.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Tharsis Republic:** Triggers their passive income and secures board control.
   - **Commercial District:** Essential for creating high-VP city clusters (e.g., the 6-city surround).
   - **Building Tag:** Can be paid for entirely with Steel, making the investment effectively "free" in terms of liquid MC.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Requirement Barrier:** You **must** be adjacent to 2 other city tiles (yours or opponents'). This prevents the card from being a Generation 1 opener unless others are playing aggressively on the board.
- **Energy Bottleneck:** Do not play this if you are already energy-starved. Tying up your energy engine in the mid-game can prevent you from trading with moons.
- **Adjacency Sniping:** Be careful not to place this next to your own cities if a greenery would provide more total points. Use it primarily to leech from opponents.

### Tips
- **Psychological Deterrent:** The mere existence of Urbanized Area in the deck forces high-level players to avoid leaving "holes" between their cities. If they do, drop this card immediately to ruin their greenery plans.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in the mid-to-late game (Generations 4-8). Essential for anyone racing for the **Mayor** milestone. Skip early if no cities are on the board.
