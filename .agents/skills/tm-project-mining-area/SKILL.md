# Project: Mining Area (#033)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 4 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Place a Mining Area tile on an area with a Steel or Titanium placement bonus that is adjacent to another of your tiles. Increase the production of that resource (Steel or Titanium) 1 step.


## Strategic Analysis (The Proximity Mine)
**Status: S-TIER OPENING / HIGH-SKILL UTILITY**

Mining Area is one of the most cost-efficient production cards in the game, but it is also the "most misread card." Its power is balanced by strict placement restrictions that require forward planning.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Extreme Efficiency:** For only 4 MC (+3 to draft), you receive 1 production (~10 MC value) and an immediate placement bonus (2-3 MC value). If played in Generation 1, it pays for itself almost instantly.
2. **Mining Guild Synergy:** This is the premier card for **Mining Guild**. Since the corporation grants a steel production whenever you gain a metal placement bonus, Mining Area provides a **double production jump** for just 4 MC.
3. **Tharsis Republic:** Can be played in Generation 1 without other tile cards, as Tharsis starts with a city already on the board.
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a Building tag and a special tile, contributing to *Builder* and *Landlord*.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Strict Placement:** You **must** place the tile adjacent to a tile you already own. This prevents it from being your first action unless you have a corporation (Tharsis) or a Prelude (e.g., *Experimental Forest*) that places a tile first.
- **Metal Only:** The tile **must** be placed on a Steel or Titanium bonus spot. You cannot place it on a generic spot just for the adjacency.
- **Late Game Decay:** In the late game, the board is often too crowded to find a valid spot for Mining Area, or the production is no longer valuable. Its value drops to "C-Tier" after Generation 5.

### Tips
- **Titanium over Steel:** Prioritize placing Mining Area on Titanium spots. Titanium production is worth ~3 MC per generation, compared to Steel's ~2 MC, and the immediate Titanium placement bonus is often more impactful for space-engine cards.
- **Blocker Play:** In the late game, use it as a cheap 4 MC "blocker" to take a high-value spot next to an opponent's city, denying them a greenery placement.

**Recommendation:** A "Must-Keep" in your starting hand if you have any way to place a tile early. High priority for metal-heavy engines. Always check for adjacency before drafting.
