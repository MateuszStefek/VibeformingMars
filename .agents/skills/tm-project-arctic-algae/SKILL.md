# Project: Arctic Algae (#033)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 12 MC
- **Tags:** Plant
- **Requirements:** Max -12°C
- **Effect:** When an Ocean tile is placed (by anyone), you gain 2 Plants. When you play this card, you gain 1 Plant.


## Strategic Analysis (The Ocean Engine)
**Status: S-TIER / BEST EARLY-GAME PLANT SOURCE**

Arctic Algae is the premier card for converting ocean placements into biological value. If played early, it provides a massive amount of "free" plants that can accelerate your greenery engine.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Massive ROI:** If all 9 oceans are placed after this card is in play, you gain 19 plants (including the 1 on play). This is worth ~2.5 Greeneries, a value of ~45 MC for a 12 MC card.
2. **Standard Project Efficiency:** It makes the "Aquifer" standard project much more viable, as you get 2 plants back with every ocean.
3. **Draft Priority:** It is a high-priority "Auto-Keep" in any opening hand that doesn't focus exclusively on a 7-8 generation rush.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Ecoline:** A legendary combo. The extra plants from oceans make Ecoline's reduced greenery cost even more oppressive.
   - **Giant Ice Asteroid (GIA):** Triggers 4 plants immediately.
   - **Miranda (Colonies):** Provides the plant tags needed to fuel high-tier animal cards.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Temperature Cap:** Keep a close eye on the temperature. In heat-heavy games, the temperature can hit -10°C before you have the 15 MC to draft and play this card.
- **Prelude Impact:** In games with *Prelude*, several oceans might be placed before the first generation. Check your opponents' preludes before committing.
- **Predator Vulnerability:** Having a constant stream of 2-4 plants makes you a target for plant-destruction cards (e.g., *Birds*, *Virus*). Time your ocean placements so you can convert to a greenery in the same action or generation.

### Tips
- **Ocean Sequencing:** If an opponent plays a card that places an ocean and removes plants (like *GIA*), they can choose the order. They will likely let you gain the plants first, then remove them. Be wary of placing oceans yourself if it puts you in the "kill range" for plant destruction.

**Recommendation:** Play as early as possible (Generation 1-2). If drawn after 4+ oceans are already on the board, calculate if the remaining 10 plants are worth the investment.
