# Project: Sabotage (#121)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 1 MC
- **Tags:** Event
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease any player's Titanium by 3, or their Steel by 4, or their MegaCredits (MC) by 7.


## Strategic Analysis (The Relationship Destroyer)
**Status: B-TIER DISRUPTION / S-TIER IN 2-PLAYER GAMES**

Sabotage is the ultimate high-tempo disruption card. While it provides zero direct benefit to your own engine, its ability to cripple an opponent's resources for a negligible cost makes it one of the most polarizing and powerful tools in competitive play.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled Denial Efficiency:** For a total investment of 4 MC (+3 to draft), you destroy up to **9 MC of value** (3 Titanium) or **7 liquid MC**. This is a massive "tempo swing" that can stall an opponent's engine by a full generation.
2. **Early Game Lethality:** Playing Sabotage in Generation 1 or 2 can prevent an opponent from affording a critical production card (*Acquired Company*) or a milestone. It is specifically devastating against **Phobolog** to strip their starting Titanium.
3. **Event Rebates:** As a Red (Event) card, it triggers rebates from **Media Group** (+3 MC) and **Interplanetary Cinematics** (+2 MC). With these active, you can actually **gain money** while destroying your opponent's resources.
4. **Milestone Control:** Use it in the final turn to drop an opponent's resource count just below yours to "snipe" the **Miner** or **Industrialist** awards.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Multplayer Dilution:** In 4-5 player games, attacking one player only helps you and the other players who weren't hit. Sabotage is strictly a 2-player or highly targeted competitive card. Only play in large games if targeting the clear runaway leader.
- **Zero Tag Value:** Provides zero progress toward requirement-based milestones like *Scientist* or *Ecologist*. It is a tactical tool, not an engine builder.
- **Target Timing:** Ensure your opponent hasn't already spent their resources. Check the player boards carefully before committing to the draft.

### Tips
- **Stealth Timing:** Like all disruption, play Sabotage *after* an opponent has taken their first action but before their second, or after they have passed, to maximize the chance that they were "saving up" for a big follow-up play that you then render impossible.

**Recommendation:** A "Must-Draft" in 2-player games. High priority if *Media Group* is in play. Excellent late-game tool for award sniping. Skip in large multiplayer games unless you need to stop a runaway winner.
