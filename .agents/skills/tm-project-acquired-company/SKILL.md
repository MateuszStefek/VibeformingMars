# Project: Acquired Company (#003)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 10 MC
- **Tags:** Earth
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 3 steps.


## Strategic Analysis (The Economic Baseline)
**Status: S-TIER / PREMIER EARLY-GAME INCOME**

Acquired Company is the "gold standard" for early-game MC production. It offers a clean, efficient return on investment that scales with Earth tag synergies.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Efficiency:** At a total cost of 13 MC (10 + 3 to draft), it pays for itself in just **5 generations**. In a standard 10-generation game, playing this in Gen 1 yields a net profit of 17 MC.
2. **Earth Tag Utility:** As one of the cheapest Earth tags with no requirements, it is a vital enabler for high-value cards like *Cartel*, *Earth Catapult*, or *Luna Metropolis*.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Teractor:** Reduces the cost to 7 MC, making the break-even point just 4 generations.
   - **Point Luna:** Triggers an immediate card draw, significantly increasing its value.
   - **Manutech:** Provides an immediate 3 MC rebate upon placement.
   - **Banker Award / Investor Award:** Helps secure these high-point awards by boosting production and tag count.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Timing Cutoff:** The "5-Generation Rule" is critical. If you are in Generation 6 of a 10-generation game, this card will only return 12 MC for a 13 MC investment. **Do not play this after Generation 5** unless you desperately need the Earth tag for a Milestone or a synergy trigger.
- **Opponent Rush:** If your opponents are terraforming rapidly (ending the game in Gen 7 or 8), the value of all production cards drops. Assess the game pace before keeping this in mid-game drafts.

### Tips
- **Earth Engine vs. Space Engine:** While space engines are flashy, a solid "Earth Engine" starting with *Acquired Company* and *Sponsors* provides the steady liquid cash needed to buy the premium cards that win games.

**Recommendation:** An "Auto-Keep" in any opening hand. High priority in the draft for Generations 1-3. Skip in the late game.
