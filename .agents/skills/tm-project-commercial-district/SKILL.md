# Project: Commercial District (#085)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 16 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease your Energy production 1 step. Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 4 steps. Place the Commercial District tile.
- **VP:** 1 VP for each adjacent City tile.


## Strategic Analysis (The City Punisher)
**Status: B-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER BLOCKER**

Commercial District is a high-utility board-control card that excels in disruption and late-game point generation. While its effective cost is high, its ability to "leech" points from opponents' cities and block prime greenery spots makes it a potent tool in competitive play.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional Board Control:** Its primary use is placing it between 2 or 3 of your **opponents' cities**. This grants you immediate VP while simultaneously "blocking" the most efficient greenery spots for those cities, effectively denying your opponents points and TR.
2. **Steel Sink for Income:** As a Building card, the 16 MC cost can be paid entirely with Steel. This allows you to convert rigid building resources into flexible liquid income and points for the endgame.
3. **Surprise Point-Slam:** In the final turn, Commercial District can be worth **3-4+ VP** if placed in a crowded city cluster (e.g., near *Noctis City* or an opponent's *Urbanized Area*).
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a Building tag and a special tile, contributing to the **Builder** and **Landlord** milestones.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Hidden Cost:** factoring in the 1 energy production penalty (~7 MC value) and the 19 MC total investment (cost + draft), you are paying **~26 MC** for 4 income. This is a very slow return on investment. **Do not play this in Generations 1-3** unless you have a specific 3-city "snatch" ready.
- **Adjacency Paradox:** Avoid placing this next to your own cities if a greenery could fit there instead. A greenery usually provides 2 total points (1 for you + 1 for the city owner), whereas the District only provides 1 VP for you.
- **Energy Scarcity:** In the *Colonies* meta, energy is vital for trading. Sacrificing energy production for MC is usually a mistake until the final turn.

### Tips
- **Ocean Sniping:** On the **Elysium** map, use Commercial District to take a spot adjacent to two oceans. This grants you a 4 MC rebate immediately, significantly improving the card's efficiency while securing board dominance.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in the mid-to-late game (Generations 5-9) if you see 2+ city clusters on the board. High priority for **Mining Guild** or anyone with a steel surplus. Skip early to preserve trading capacity.
