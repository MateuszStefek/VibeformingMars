# Corporation: Helion

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Starting MC:** 42
- **Heat Production:** 3
- **Ability:** You may use heat as MegaCredits.

## Strategic Analysis (The Thermal Hybrid)
**Status: B-TIER / HYBRID**

Helion is a unique corporation that blurs the line between energy/heat and currency. This flexibility allows for interesting early-game tempo plays, though it can struggle if it doesn't commit to a clear strategy.

### Why it is a viable play:
1. **Early Game Tempo:** Starting with 3 heat production is effectively starting with +3 MC production that can also be used to raise temperature. This allows Helion to play more cards in the crucial first generations.
2. **Temperature Rushing:** Helion can pivot to a pure terraforming rush if it gets enough energy or heat production cards.
3. **Late Game Value:** Even after the temperature is maxed, Helion's heat remains useful as currency, preventing the "dead resource" problem other players face.

### Synergies:
- **Colonies (Titan/Io/Europa):** Colonies that provide heat or energy are exceptionally valuable for Helion.
- **Mohole Area / Soletta:** These high-yield heat production cards become dual-purpose for Helion.
- **Physics Complex:** High energy production can be converted to heat (MC) or used for the Physics Complex action for VPs.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
High priority for 'Mayor' and 'Gardener'. Use the early game to claim the double-ocean placement bonus for cards/plants.
### Hellas
Relevance of the South Pole for heat bonuses. Target 'Rim Settler' if playing space tags, or 'Polar Explorer' for easy adjacency points.
### Elysium
Focus on the 3-plant placement bonus. 'Generalist' is an easy milestone if you can balance production early.

### Tips
- **The Heat Trap:** Don't be afraid to spend your heat as MC early on. The tempo gained from an extra 'Sponsors' or 'Acquired Company' is often worth more than 1/8th of a temperature step.
- **Pivot Point:** Watch the temperature track. Once it's near max, ensure you have a plan for your excess heat (usually just using it as MC).

**Recommendation:** A solid, flexible choice. Best picked when you have energy-to-heat conversion cards or when playing with the Colonies expansion.
