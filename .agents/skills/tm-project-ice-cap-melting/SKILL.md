# Project: Ice Cap Melting (#181)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 5 MC
- **Tags:** None (Event)
- **Requirements:** Min +2°C
- **Effect:** Place 1 Ocean tile.


## Strategic Analysis (The Late-Game Bargain)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / SITUATIONAL "CLEAN-UP" CARD**

Ice Cap Melting is one of the most cost-efficient cards in the game, but it is strictly limited by the pacing of global parameters. While it offers a "bargain" price for an ocean, the requirement often arrives too late to be useful in competitive play.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Unrivaled Efficiency:** For only 5 MC (+3 to draft), you gain 1 TR (from the ocean) and a placement bonus. This 8 MC total investment is the cheapest way to place an ocean, representing a 10 MC savings over the Standard Project.
2. **Synergies:**
   - **Arctic Algae:** Gain 2 plants for a very low cost, helping complete a final greenery.
   - **Media Group:** As an Event card, it triggers the 3 MC rebate, making the ocean effectively cost only 2 MC (plus draft).
   - **Vitor:** Triggers the 3 MC rebate for cards with VP icons (wait, it has no VP icon). Unlike *Research*, this card does not trigger Vitor.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Requirement Barrier:** The +2°C requirement is the card's greatest weakness. In most games, all 9 ocean tiles are placed *long before* the temperature reaching this point. **Never keep this in your opening hand.**
- **Late-Game Dead-Weight:** Monitor the ocean track. If 8 oceans are already placed and temperature is at -10°C, discard this card. It is highly likely another player will Standard Project the final ocean before the requirement is met.
- **Solo Mode Utility:** In Solo play, it is much more reliable, as you can control the parameter pacing to ensure this cheap TR hits the board in the final generations.

### Tips
- **Requirement Cheating:** Use **Special Design** or the **Inventrix** corporation to play Ice Cap Melting at -2°C or 0°C. This allows you to snag the final high-value ocean placement bonuses (like 2 card draws) before your opponents can react.

**Recommendation:** Generally a "Pass" in drafts unless you see a clear path to early heat production or possess requirement-bypassing cards. Excellent as a filler in the final generations if oceans are still available. Skip in high-player games where oceans disappear rapidly.
