# Project: Heavy Equipment (#116)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 13 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your Steel production 2 steps.


## Strategic Analysis (The Building Engine Foundation)
**Status: B-TIER UTILITY / EARLY-GAME STAPLE**

Heavy Equipment is a foundational production card for metal-heavy strategies. It provides a reliable, mid-tier boost to your building potential, allowing you to fund expensive infrastructure projects without draining your liquid MC.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional ROI:** For 13 MC (+3 to draft), you gain 2 Steel production (worth 4 MC per generation). This yields a **4-generation break-even point**. In a standard game, playing this in Generations 1-3 results in a massive net profit of 20-30+ MC in value.
2. **Steel Sink Catalyst:** It provides the raw resources needed to play high-value building-tag cards like *Research Outpost*, *Strip Mine*, or *Development Center* for "free" in terms of liquid cash.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Advanced Alloys:** The premier pairing. If your steel is worth 3 MC, Heavy Equipment's yield jumps to 6 MC per generation, making it one of the most efficient ROI cards in the game.
   - **Mining Guild:** Triggers the corporation's passive and contributes to their massive steel-based engine.
   - **Manutech:** Triggers an immediate resource gain upon increasing production.
   - **Builder Milestone:** Provides a Building tag and the production needed to keep playing more building tags to win the race.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **The Steel Trap:** Over-investing in steel production is only useful if you have "sinks" (cards to spend it on). If your hand is full of space tags or events, Heavy Equipment is a waste of capital.
- **Opportunity Cost:** If you have to choose between Heavy Equipment and a card like *Acquired Company* in Gen 1, the liquid MC production is usually more flexible and therefore slightly better.
- **Late Game Decay:** Like all production cards, its value drops after Generation 5. Only draft late if you need the Building tag to hit a specific requirement or milestone.

### Tips
- **Tempo over Production:** Do not play this card if it leaves you with zero MC to spend on card draw or requirements. It is a "long game" investment; ensure your immediate tempo is stable before committing the 16 MC.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in Generations 1-3. Essential for **Mining Guild** or anyone holding **Advanced Alloys**. Skip in the late game.
