# Corporation: Teractor

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Starting MC:** 60
- **Ability:** When you play a card with an Earth tag, you pay 3 MC less for it.

## Strategic Analysis (The Economic Powerhouse)
**Status: A/S-TIER / RELIABLE**

Teractor is the safest and most consistent corporation in the game. With the highest starting MC of any corporation and a discount on one of the most powerful tag types, Teractor is built for massive early-game snowballs.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Gen 1 Snowball:** 60 MC allows Teractor to buy and play almost any combination of production cards in the first generation.
2. **Earth Tag Synergy:** Earth tags are synonymous with economy (e.g., 'Sponsors', 'Cartels', 'Acquired Company'). Teractor makes these already strong cards even better.
3. **Versatility:** With its massive cash reserves, Teractor can pivot into any strategy (Board, Engine, or Terraforming) based on its draw.

### Synergies:
- **Earth Tags:** 'Sponsors', 'Acquired Company', 'Cartels', 'Earth Office', and 'Lunar Beam' are all high-priority cards.
- **Milestones:** Teractor is the king of the 'Generalist' and 'Builder' milestones due to its high starting capital.
- **Earth Office:** Further reduces the cost of Earth tags, making them incredibly cheap or even free.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
Strong for 'Terraformer'. High starting capital allows for early milestone locking.
### Hellas
Good for 'Diversifier' if you can branch into different productions early.
### Elysium
High synergy with 'Legend' milestone due to frequent event plays.

### Tips
- **Don't Overthink It:** Teractor's strength is its simplicity. Focus on building your MC production in the first 3 generations to ensure you can afford anything in the late game.
- **Milestone Race:** Use your Gen 1 cash to secure a milestone early. Teractor is often the fastest to reach 8 building tags or diverse production.

**Recommendation:** The best "fallback" pick. High priority if you have 2+ Earth-tagged economy cards in your starting hand.
