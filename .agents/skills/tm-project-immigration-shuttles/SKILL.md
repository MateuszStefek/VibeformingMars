# Project: Immigration Shuttles (#031)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 31 MC
- **Tags:** Earth, Space
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 5 steps.
- **VP:** 1 VP for every 3 Cities in play (all players).


## Strategic Analysis (The Late-Game Multiplier)
**Status: C-TIER ECONOMY / SITUATIONAL "POINT-SLAM"**

Immigration Shuttles is a high-cost hybrid card that is often a "trap" for newer players. While its massive income boost looks impressive, its high opportunity cost and board-dependent VP make it difficult to justify in the early game compared to more efficient production cards.

### Why it is a good play (rarely):
1. **Titanium Sink:** As a Space card, it can be paid for with Titanium. This is the premier way for titanium-rich players (like **Phobolog**) to "liquidate" their metal into long-term income and points.
2. **Double Tag Synergy:** Provides both an **Earth** and a **Space** tag.
   - **Earth:** Triggers *Point Luna* and benefits from *Earth Office*.
   - **Space:** Benefits from *Space Station* and *Research Outpost* discounts.
3. **Surprise Point-Slam:** In high-player games (4-5 players) where cities are abundant, this card acts like a "Jovian Multiplier" for cities. If there are 15 cities on the board, this card is worth **5 VP**, making the 34 MC investment much more palatable.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Poor Early Math:** At 34 MC total investment, it takes **7 generations** to break even on the production alone. If played after Generation 3, it will likely lose money compared to simple terraforming.
- **2-Player Inefficiency:** In 1v1 games, cities are often sparse. If fewer than 9 cities are placed total, the point value is too low to justify the 31 MC cost.
- **Opportunity Cost:** The 31 MC spent here could fund three Standard Project greeneries or multiple high-value engine pieces that provide better long-term scaling.

### Tips
- **Unity Party (Turmoil):** Prioritize playing this when the **Unity** party is ruling to maximize the value of the Titanium spent. Use it as a point-sink in the final generation if you have excess metal and a crowded board.

**Recommendation:** A low-priority draft for most players. High priority for **Phobolog** or **Teractor** in Generations 1-2. Skip early in low-player games. Use as a points dump in the final round if the city count is high.
