# Project: Energy Tapping (#055)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 3 MC
- **Tags:** Power
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Decrease any player's Energy production 1 step. Increase your Energy production 1 step.
- **VP:** -1


## Strategic Analysis (The Zero-Sum Disruptor)
**Status: A-TIER IN 2-PLAYER / S-TIER IN COLONIES**

Energy Tapping is one of the most efficient disruption cards in the game. It provides ultra-cheap energy production while simultaneously stalling an opponent's engine. While the -1 VP penalty is present, its impact on tempo and denial often outweighs the point loss.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled Cost Efficiency:** For a total investment of 6 MC (3 cost + 3 to draft), you gain 1 energy production. This is nearly 50% cheaper than a 11 MC Standard Project Power Plant.
2. **Critical Denial:**
   - **Colonies Trade Kill:** Energy is the currency for trading. Stealing an opponent's 3rd energy production can prevent them from trading with high-value colonies like *Luna* or *Ceres*, a potentially game-winning blow.
   - **Requirement Sabotage:** Can knock an opponent below the threshold for *AI Central* (3 energy) or *Physics Complex* (6 energy).
3. **2-Player Powerhouse:** In 1v1 games, it is a direct 2-step production swing. You aren't just gaining a resource; you are actively widening the gap between you and your only competitor.
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a Power tag, contributing to the *Diversifier* milestone or requirements for cards like *Power Supply Consortium*.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Mandatory Target:** You **must** be able to decrease someone's energy production. If everyone is at 0, the card is unplayable. 
- **VP Penalty:** In a very tight, high-VP game, that -1 can be the difference between winning and losing. Only play it early where the energy income can generate more than 1 point of value over time.
- **4-5 Player Dilution:** In large games, attacking one player only helps you and the other 2-3 players who weren't hit. Use it tactically on the leader rather than indiscriminately.

### Tips
- **Stalling:** Like all action cards, use Energy Tapping as your first move to "pass time" and see if an opponent increases their energy production, providing you with a fresh target to tap.

**Recommendation:** An "Auto-Keep" in Generations 1-3 if you have plans for cities or trading. High priority for **Thorgate** or **Aridor**. Excellent in Solo mode. Skip only in the final turns when points are more valuable than production.
