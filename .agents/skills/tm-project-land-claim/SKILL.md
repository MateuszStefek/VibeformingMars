# Project: Land Claim (#066)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 1 MC
- **Tags:** Event
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Place a Land Claim marker on any non-reserved area. Only you may place a tile on that area.


## Strategic Analysis (The Board Reserve)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER IN 2-PLAYER GAMES**

Land Claim is a niche tactical card used primarily for board control and resource denial. While it provides zero direct engine value, its ability to "reserve" high-value map locations makes it a potent tool in competitive or 2-player formats.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Exceptional Efficiency with Synergies:** For only 1 MC (+3 to draft), it is an Event card. If you have **Media Group** (+3 MC) or **Interplanetary Cinematics** (+2 MC), playing Land Claim is actually **profitable**. It becomes a "no-brainer" to play just for the cash injection and a minor board advantage.
2. **Denial Power:** In 1v1 games, reserving a high-value spot (e.g., a 2-VP city location or a spot with 3 plant bonuses) is effectively a 2-3 point swing. You are preventing your opponent's most efficient play while securing your own.
3. **Strategic "Dibs":** Use it to reserve a spot you cannot afford to build on yet, preventing an opponent from "sniping" a location you've set up with greeneries.
4. **Milestone Padding:** One of the cheapest ways to add an "Event" tag to your count for the **Legend** milestone (Elysium map).

### The "Trap" Check:
- **No Immediate Bonus:** You do **not** receive placement bonuses (plants, steel, etc.) when you play the marker. You only get them later when you actually place a tile there.
- **No Milestone Progress:** The marker does **not** count as a tile. It does not contribute to the *Landlord* or *Mayor* milestones.
- **Reserved Area Restriction:** You **cannot** play Land Claim on "Reserved Areas" such as Ocean spots, the *Noctis City* reserve, or orbital cities (*Phobos/Ganymede*).
- **Opportunity Cost:** If you aren't playing with event synergies, the 4 MC (card + draft) is usually better spent on establishes your production engine.

### Tips
- **Ocean Walk Blocking:** Use Land Claim to occupy a spot adjacent to multiple ocean tiles. This denies opponents the 2-6 MC placement bonus they would get for placing a tile there later, effectively "starving" their board economy.

**Recommendation:** A low-to-mid priority draft. High priority if you have *Media Group* or are playing a 2-player game. Use tactically to reserve critical board real estate. Skip early in large multiplayer games.
