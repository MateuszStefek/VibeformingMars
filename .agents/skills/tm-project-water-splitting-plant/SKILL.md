# Project: Water Splitting Plant (#177)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 12 MC
- **Tags:** Building
- **Requirements:** 2 Ocean Tiles.
- **Action:** Spend 3 Energy to raise Oxygen 1 step.


## Strategic Analysis (The Oxygen Alternative)
**Status: B-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER IN SOLO MODE**

Water Splitting Plant is a situational "industrial" card that converts surplus power into Oxygen progress. While often considered the weakest of the "bumpers" in multiplayer, its ability to move the oxygen track without relying on plants makes it a vital win-condition in Solo play.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Oxygen Control:** Provides a reliable way to raise Oxygen that doesn't depend on board state or biological production. Use it to "snipe" oxygen thresholds or to hasten the game's end to deny engine-builders more generations.
2. **Solo Mode Essential:** Consistently rated as a must-buy in Solo play. Oxygen is often the hardest parameter to reach without lucky plant draws; Water Splitting Plant provides a non-random path to victory.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **UNMI:** The premier synergy. Provides a cheap, repeatable way to trigger UNMI's corporation ability (+1 TR when your TR increases) in the very first generations.
   - **Mass Converter:** Science engines often overproduce energy; the Splitting Plant provides a way to trade that "dead" production for TR and global progress.
   - **Credicor:** Triggers the 4 MC rebate for high-cost cards (Wait, it costs 12 MC, so no rebate. Correction: Credicor only rebates 20+ MC cards).

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Efficiency Paradox:** Compared to *Steelworks* or *Ore Processor*, Water Splitting Plant is inefficient because it provides zero resources (steel/titanium) in addition to the TR. You must use the action at least **5 times** to break even on the investment.
- **Colonies Trade-off:** In games with *Colonies*, energy is at a premium for trading. Spending 3 energy for 1 TR is almost always inferior to trading with a colony for cards or MC.
- **Requirement Barrier:** The 2-ocean requirement can arrive late in slow games. Do not hold this from Generation 1 unless you have a path to early oceans.

### Tips
- **Late Game Pivot:** Only play Water Splitting Plant if you have 3+ energy production and zero other use for it. In the final generations, if your trading fleets are done, it provides a way to squeeze out a final TR and end the game on your terms.

**Recommendation:** A mid-to-low priority draft in multiplayer. High priority in **Solo Mode** or if playing **UNMI**. Skip if playing with *Colonies* unless your energy engine is already over-producing.
