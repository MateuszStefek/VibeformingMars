# Project: Advanced Alloys (#025)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 9 MC
- **Tags:** Science
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Each unit of Steel you use is worth 1 MC more. Each unit of Titanium you use is worth 1 MC more.


## Strategic Analysis (The Value Multiplier)
**Status: S-TIER / ENGINE STARTER**

Advanced Alloys is a high-ceiling card that transforms your resource economy. It makes every building and space card significantly cheaper if you have metal production.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Resource Efficiency:** Steel becomes worth 3 MC and Titanium worth 4 MC. This represents a 50% (Steel) to 33% (Titanium) increase in their purchasing power.
2. **Science Tag:** It provides a Science tag with no requirements, which is vital for unlocking high-tier cards like AI Central and Mars University.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Phobolog / Interplanetary Cinematics:** Massive immediate value for corporations that start with metals or high metal production.
   - **Unity (Turmoil):** Combined with the Unity party bonus, titanium can reach a value of 5+ MC per unit.
   - **Space/Building Engines:** Essential for strategies focusing on Jovian tags, cities, or industrial projects.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Card Draw Dependency:** You must have cards to spend your metals on. If you boost the value of metals but don't draw building/space tags, you end up with a useless stockpile.
- **Break-Even Point:** You need to spend a total of 12 units of metal (Steel + Titanium) to recoup the 12 MC cost (9 + 3 to buy). Ensure you have enough production to hit this early.

### Tips
- **Stacking Alloys:** If you find *Mercurian Alloys* later, you can stack the effects for "Super-Titanium." In competitive 2-player games, this engine can often overcome even the fastest terraforming rush.

**Recommendation:** Play early (Gen 1-3) if you have *any* metal production or need the Science tag. In the late game, only play if you have a massive stockpile of metals and a hand full of tags to spend them on.
