# Project: Mars University (#073)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 8 MC
- **Tags:** Science, Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** When you play a Science tag (including this one), you may discard a card from your hand to draw a card.
- **VP:** 1


## Strategic Analysis (The Deck Filter)
**Status: S-TIER / BEST-IN-CLASS ENGINE PIECE**

Mars University is arguably the most efficient card-filtering engine in the game. It provides unparalleled "agency" over the deck by allowing you to swap "junk" cards for potential high-value plays.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled Efficiency:** For only 8 MC (+3 to buy), you get a Science tag, a Building tag (can pay with steel), 1 VP, and a powerful triggered effect.
2. **Card Quality Control:** It turns every future Science tag into a "reroll" of the deck. This is critical for finding specific game-enders or requirements you're currently missing.
3. **Trigger on Self:** The effect triggers when you play Mars University itself, providing an immediate swap if you have a card to discard.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **AI Central / Development Center:** These cards give you raw volume; Mars University gives you the quality to filter that volume.
   - **Olympus Conference:** Pairs perfectly for a "Science Draw Engine." (Rule of thumb: Play Mars University *first* to find better targets).
   - **Vitor / Valley Trust:** Corporations that thrive on finding specific synergies quickly.

### Advanced Tactical Tips (2025 Meta):
- **Fueling the Engine:** Sometimes it is worth buying a 3 MC card during the research phase specifically to have a discard target for a Mars University trigger.
- **Sequencing:** If you have multiple Science tags, play Mars University first to maximize the number of "swaps" you get.
- **Stealth Play:** In competitive matches, don't play it immediately if you don't have follow-up Science tags. If you reveal it too early, opponents will "hate-draft" Science tags away from you.

### When to Skip:
- **Extreme Rush:** If the game is ending very quickly (Generation 7 or 8) and you have zero Science tags in hand or no "dead" cards to filter, the 11 MC might be better spent on direct terraforming.

**Recommendation:** An absolute "Auto-Keep" in almost every opening hand. Priority 1 for Steel and MC investment in the early game.
