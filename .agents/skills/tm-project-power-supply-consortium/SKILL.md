# Project: Power Supply Consortium (#056)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 5 MC
- **Tags:** Power
- **Requirements:** 2 Power Tags.
- **Effect:** Decrease any player's Energy production 1 step. Increase your Energy production 1 step.


## Strategic Analysis (The Zero-Sum Disruptor)
**Status: A-TIER DISRUPTION / S-TIER IN COLONIES**

Power Supply Consortium is one of the most impactful disruption cards in the game. While it requires an established power infrastructure, its ability to steal energy production makes it a premier tool for stalling opponents' engines and securing a dominant trading position.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled Denial Power:** Stealing 1 energy production is a double-swing. You gain ~7 MC of value while an opponent loses the same.
2. **Colony Trade Kill:** In the 2024-2025 meta, this is the card's most devastating use. By dropping an opponent from 3 energy production to 2, you force them to either pay 9 MC to trade next generation or skip trading entirely—a massive economic blow.
3. **Engine Sabotage:** Use it to "turn off" high-value actions like **AI Central** (requires 1 energy) or to knock a player below the 6-energy threshold for **Physics Complex**.
4. **Milestone Support:** Provides a Power tag, contributing toward the **Diversifier** milestone and requirement-stacking for other power cards.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Requirement Constraint:** You **must** already have 2 Power tags in play. This prevents the card from being an early-game opener unless you have a Power-heavy corporation (*Thorgate*) or Preludes.
- **Multplayer Dilution:** In 4-5 player games, attacking one player only helps you and the other 2-3 players who weren't hit. Use it selectively on the leader or a player directly competing with you for a milestone.
- **Target Availability:** You **must** be able to decrease someone's energy production. If everyone is at 0, the card is unplayable.

### Tips
- **Stalling:** Like all action-denial cards, use Power Supply Consortium *after* an opponent has passed. This prevents them from reacting by buying more power in the same generation, ensuring they are "locked out" of their trade or action in the next generation.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft in 2-player games or if playing with *Colonies*. High priority for **Thorgate**. Skip early if you have zero Power tags. Use tactically to "break" an opponent's threshold.
