# Corporation: Palladin Shipping

## Corp Data

- **Included in:** Prelude 2
- **Starting MC:** 36
- **Starting Resources:** 5 Titanium
- **Tags:** Space
- **Ability (Effect):** When you play a space event, gain 1 titanium.
- **Action:** Spend 2 titanium to raise temperature 1 step.

## Strategic Analysis (The Orbital Heater)
**Status: LOW TIER**

Palladin Shipping attempts to bridge the gap between Space-heavy economic strategies and temperature-based terraforming rush. While it provides a unique outlet for excess titanium, its starting capital is low, making for a slow start.

### Why it is a viable play:
1. **Titanium Flexibility:** Titanium is usually restricted to space cards. Palladin turns it into temperature steps (and TR), providing a guaranteed use for titanium in every generation.
2. **Event Rebates:** Gaining 1 titanium for every space event is a decent rebate that adds up over a long game.
3. **TR Rush:** If you can generate massive amounts of titanium (e.g., through 'Advanced Alloys'), Palladin can end the game very quickly by maxing out the temperature track.

### Synergies:
- **Advanced Alloys:** Increases the value of your titanium, making the Temperature action even more efficient.
- **Deimos Down / Big Asteroid:** High-value space events that now provide an extra titanium rebate.
- **Saturn Systems:** If both are in the game, the prevalence of space tags benefits Palladin's event-trigger ability.

## Map Analysis
### Tharsis (Base Map)
High priority for 'Mayor' and 'Gardener'. Use the early game to claim the double-ocean placement bonus for cards/plants.
### Hellas
Relevance of the South Pole for heat bonuses. Target 'Rim Settler' if playing space tags, or 'Polar Explorer' for easy adjacency points.
### Elysium
Focus on the 3-plant placement bonus. 'Generalist' is an easy milestone if you can balance production early.

### Tips
- **Titanium over MC:** Prioritize titanium production over MC production if you have temperature-based TR goals.
- **Temp Control:** Use your action to "steal" temperature thresholds (like +1 heat production or +1 ocean) from opponents who are saving up heat.

**Recommendation:** Low tier. Often outclassed by 'Helion' or 'Phobolog'. Only pick if you have a hand full of high-yield space events or early titanium production.
