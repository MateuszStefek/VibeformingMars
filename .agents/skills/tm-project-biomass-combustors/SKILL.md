# Project: Biomass Combustors (#155)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 4 MC
- **Tags:** Power, Building
- **Requirements:** 6% Oxygen
- **Effect:** Increase your Energy production 2 steps. Decrease any player's Plant production 1 step.
- **VP:** -1


## Strategic Analysis (The Late-Game Energy Dump)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / SITUATIONAL OFFENSE**

Biomass Combustors is a niche card used primarily to convert excess building resources into energy or to disrupt a runaway plant engine. While statistically efficient for energy yield, its requirements and VP penalty make it a difficult fit for most strategies.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **Steel Sink for Energy:** As a Building card, it is one of the few ways to turn late-game steel into energy. If you draw a high-value city card that requires power, this is a very cheap (4 MC) way to enable that play.
2. **Disruptive Plant Hate:** Highly effective in 2-player games or against corporations like **Ecoline**. Removing 1 plant production can delay an opponent's greenery conversion, potentially denying them a milestone or placement bonus.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Robotic Workforce:** Can be used to double the energy yield (+4 total), though this also doubles the VP penalty.
   - **Inventrix:** Allows you to play the card before the 6% Oxygen mark, making the energy production more impactful for mid-game engine building.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Oxygen Barrier:** By the time Oxygen hits 6% (typically Generation 5-8), most early-game energy needs are already solved. Playing it late for energy production often feels redundant.
- **Victory Point Penalty:** The -1 VP is a significant price to pay in a competitive game. Ensure that the 2 energy steps or the plant disruption provides at least 5+ MC of value to justify the point loss.
- **Solo Mode Inefficiency:** In Solo play, the plant destruction is useless, and the late-game timing makes the energy production less valuable for the terraforming rush.

### Tips
- **Adjacency Blocking:** Use the low cost (4 MC) and Building tag to quickly drop this card to satisfy a "number of cards played" requirement or to trigger a building discount before playing a more expensive city.

**Recommendation:** Generally a "Pass" in drafts. Only keep if you are desperate for energy to fuel a late-game city or if you need to slow down an opponent's plant engine. Skip if Oxygen is nowhere near 6%.
