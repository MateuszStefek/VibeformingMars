# Project: Energy Saving (#054)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 15 MC
- **Tags:** Power
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your Energy production 1 step for each City tile in play.


## Strategic Analysis (The Scalable Power Source)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER IN 4-5 PLAYER GAMES**

Energy Saving is a high-ceiling production card that is strictly limited by the board state. While it is often "dead weight" in the early game, it is one of the most efficient ways to generate massive energy in the mid-to-late game, especially in matches with many players or city-focused corporations.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Unrivaled Scaling:** For 15 MC (+3 to draft), it can provide 4-6+ energy production in the late game. This is significantly more efficient than standard energy projects or individual power cards.
2. **Instant "Engine Starter":** Perfect for "turning on" high-requirement energy cards like **Physics Complex** (6 energy) or **Mass Converter** in a single action, often catching opponents off guard.
3. **Colonies Enabler:** In the 2024-2025 meta, Energy Saving is a premier tool for powering a second or third trading ship. Since trading requires 3 energy, this card often provides exactly what is needed once a few cities hit the board.
4. **Award Stealing:** Frequently used to steal the **Thermalist** or **Industrialist** awards by providing a sudden, massive jump in production that an opponent cannot easily counter.
5. **Synergies:**
   - **Tharsis Republic:** Cities hit the board earlier, making Energy Saving viable as early as Generation 3-4.
   - **Community Services:** Provides MC production for cards with no tags (wait, it has a Power tag).

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Early Game Stalling:** **Never keep this in your starting hand.** It ties up 3 MC for several generations while you wait for the board to populate.
- **2-Player Inefficiency:** In 1v1 games, cities are often sparse. Unless one player is heavily committed to a city strategy, this card rarely reaches its efficiency breakpoint.
- **The "Rush" Meta:** If opponents are terraforming quickly (ending the game via heat/oceans), there may never be enough cities to make this card worth the investment.

### Tips
- **City Count Threshold:** Aim to play this when there are at least **4 cities** in play. This yields a ~4.5 MC per energy ratio, which is the standard efficiency mark for power cards.

**Recommendation:** A mid-priority draft in the mid-game (Generations 4-7). High priority for city-heavy games or if you hold *Physics Complex*. Skip early or in slow 2-player games.
