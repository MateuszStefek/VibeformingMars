# Project: Power Infrastructure (#194)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Corporate Era)
- **Cost:** 4 MC
- **Tags:** Power, Building
- **Requirements:** None
- **Action:** Spend any amount of Energy to gain the same amount of MegaCredits (MC).


## Strategic Analysis (The Resource Sink)
**Status: C-TIER UTILITY / S-TIER ENGINE STALLER**

Power Infrastructure is a situational utility card used primarily to manipulate resource flow and game pacing. While inefficient for direct income, its ability to convert flexible energy into flexible currency makes it a powerful tool for late-game liquidity and engine management.

### Why it is a good play:
1. **The "Engine Stall":** Its primary high-level use is preventing the game from ending. By converting energy to MC instead of letting it turn into heat, you prevent the temperature from rising. This buys your economy more generations to outscale "rush" strategies.
2. **Exceptional Liquidity:** It turns energy into a universal currency. In the late game, if you have massive energy generators like **Mass Converter**, this card provides the infinite cash needed to fund high-VP cities and greeneries.
3. **Double Tag Synergy:** Provides both a **Power** and a **Building** tag for only 4 MC (+3 to draft). This is the cheapest way to hit the 8-building requirement for the **Builder** milestone or to trigger building discounts.
4. **Synergies:**
   - **Thorgate:** Benefits from the Power tag discount, making the card nearly free.
   - **Callisto (Colonies):** If you are trading for energy, Power Infrastructure ensures that any leftover power is never wasted.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Efficiency Paradox:** In most cases, letting energy become heat is better than converting it to MC. Heat raises TR, which provides permanent income *and* a victory point. Converting 1 energy to 1 MC is a statistically poor trade unless the game's temperature is already maxed.
- **Early Game Dead-Weight:** Do not play this in Generations 1-3. You rarely have the excess energy to justify the action, and the 7 MC total investment is better spent on establishing production.
- **Action Economy:** Like all action cards, it consumes one of your two actions per turn.

### Tips
- **Milestone Snipe:** Use the sudden cash injection from a large energy dump to fund a contested award (like **Banker**) in the final turn. It is one of the hardest moves for an opponent to block.

**Recommendation:** A low-priority draft for most players. High priority if building a massive energy/science engine or if playing **Thorgate**. Skip if you are pursuing a "Rush" strategy.
