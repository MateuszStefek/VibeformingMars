# Project: Inventors' Guild (#006)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 9 MC
- **Tags:** Science
- **Requirements:** None
- **Action:** Look at the top card of the deck. You may either buy it for 3 MC or discard it.


## Strategic Analysis (The Precision Filter)
**Status: A-TIER / DECK FILTER ENGINE**

Inventors' Guild is a high-value filtering tool that allows you to "dig" for win conditions without clogging your hand. While it is slower than raw draw engines, its Science tag and optional purchase make it a staple for engine builders.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Quality over Quantity:** Unlike *Restricted Area*, you only spend the 3 MC if you actually want the card. This saves you money in the long run by filtering out "junk" late-game or cards you can't afford.
2. **Science Tag Enabler:** Provides a Science tag with no requirements, which is essential for hitting the 3-tag threshold for **AI Central**.
3. **Synergies:**
   - **Mars University:** The combination of filtering with the Guild and swapping with the University provides almost complete control over your hand quality.
   - **Science pay-off cards:** Essential for strategies focusing on *Antigravity Technology* or *Mass Converter*.
   - **Vitor:** The low cost and science tag fit perfectly into Vitor's "points and production" strategy.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Upfront Cost:** 12 MC (9 to play + 3 to draft) is a significant early-game investment. If playing it prevents you from building basic MC or heat production in Generations 1-2, it might be too slow.
- **The "Slow Game" Requirement:** In fast-paced games (especially with 4+ players or heavy *Prelude* usage), the game might end before the filtering pays off. Its value is highest in 2-player or engine-heavy 3-player games.

### Tips
- **Mid-Game Pivot:** If you have established your economy by Generation 4 but haven't found your "win condition" (e.g., Jovian multipliers or a specific plant engine), Inventors' Guild is the best tool to find it. 
- **Tempo Passing:** Like other action cards, use the Guild action first in your turn to "stall" and see if opponents raise global parameters that give you a threshold bonus.

**Recommendation:** A high-priority draft if you are pursuing a Science strategy or playing a 2-player game. In a 4-5 player "Rush" game, prioritize immediate production instead.
