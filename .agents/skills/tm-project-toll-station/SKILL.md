# Project: Toll Station (#096)

## Card Data
- **Included in:** Base Game (Basic)
- **Cost:** 12 MC
- **Tags:** Space
- **Requirements:** None
- **Effect:** Increase your MegaCredit (MC) production 1 step for each Space tag your opponents have in play.


## Strategic Analysis (The Opponent Leech)
**Status: S-TIER IN 4-5 PLAYER GAMES / B-TIER UTILITY**

Toll Station is a high-ceiling economic card that rewards you for your opponents' industrial specialization. While inconsistent in small games, its ability to generate massive income from a crowded space board makes it a game-defining card in high-player count matches.

### Why it is a top-tier play:
1. **Exceptional Scaling:** In a 4-5 player game, opponents will naturally accumulate 10-15+ Space tags. This turns a 12 MC investment (+3 draft) into a **+10 MC production monster**, far exceeding the efficiency of any other economic card.
2. **The "Banker" Sniper:** Its primary competitive use is stealing the **Banker** award. By dropping Toll Station in the late game, you can suddenly increase your production by 8-12 steps in a single action, often catching opponents off guard who thought they had a safe lead.
3. **Titanium Sink:** As a Space card, it can be paid for with Titanium. This allows you to convert rigid resources into flexible liquid income without draining your MC reserves.
4. **Requirement Bridge:** Provides a requirement-free Space tag, which helps you reach the threshold for high-tier cards like *Quantum Extractor* or *Mass Converter*.

### The "Trap" Check:
- **Solo & 2-Player Inefficiency:** In Solo play, it provides **zero production**. In 1v1 games, it is rarely efficient unless your opponent is heavily committed to a space engine. Aim for at least **+4 production** for the card to be worth the 15 MC total investment.
- **Early Game Uncertainty:** Playing this in Generation 1-2 is usually a gamble. Opponents may see your Toll Station and intentionally avoid space tags to "starve" your engine. It is often better to hold it until the mid-game (Generations 4-6).
- **No Income on Self:** Remember that it only counts **opponent** tags. Your own space tags do not contribute to the production jump.

### Tips
- **Unity Party (Turmoil):** Prioritize playing this when the **Unity** party is ruling to maximize the value of the Titanium spent. Use the sudden cash injection to fund high-VP cities or greeneries in the final round.

**Recommendation:** A "Must-Draft" in 4-5 player games. High priority if an opponent is playing **Point Luna** or **Saturn Systems**. Skip early in 2-player games unless you see a clear path to 4+ opponent space tags.
